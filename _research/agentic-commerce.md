## Overview

This research examines the structural impact of **Agentic Commerce** on incumbent e-commerce platforms, focusing on how autonomous AI agents change discovery, transaction, and fulfillment workflows.

## Key Questions

- How are major LLM players entering commerce via agent interfaces?
- Where do left-side SaaS transaction opportunities emerge across verticals?
- What governance and customer-service depth differentiate platform resilience?

## Methodology

- Comparative analysis of global LLM commerce entry strategies
- Long-tail supply chain integration mapping
- Platform governance and CS depth benchmarking

## Findings

Agentic Commerce is most disruptive where platforms lack embedded workflow automation and vertical-specific transaction infrastructure. Left-side SaaS layers capturing agent-to-merchant orchestration represent the highest-conviction opportunity set.
